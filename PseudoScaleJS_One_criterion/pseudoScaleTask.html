Greetings

In this task you get to explore how small differences in frequency (pitch) you are able to hear.

During the experimental task you will be presented with a screen like this:

By clicking on lower part (grey) the screen you will be presented with a stimulus. Each stimulus is a pair of pure tones, the first of which is always the same across all stimuli but the second may be higher than the first. Your task is to try to detect if there was a difference between them.

There is a practice task during which you have the chance to acquaint yourself with how the stimuli sound.

Responses are given by clicking Yes or No in a dialog box that will open near where you clicked:

Unlike in a typical task of this kind, you have a say in how large frequency differences will be presented to you. The higher you click on the screen the larger the frequency difference will be; horizontal location does not affect the stimulus in any way. But there's a catch—quite literally!

Every now and then you will be presented with random catch trials. During these trials there is either

  1. no difference between the tones, or
  2. a random difference that is smaller than what you picked.

This is so that you, as a participant, would have to use your ears and not just your eyes in the task, and so that there would be some challenge to it. But in any case: keep your ears peeled on each trial!

During the task you should also be moving the red horizontal bar by dragging it: double tap and hold if you are using a touchpad or just hold down the left mouse button to get a hold of the bar. You should try to drag it as close as possible to where you think your threshold for detecting frequency differences lies. But there's a catch here too!

Below a random point on the screen the frequency difference will always be zero. So when you are moving the threshold bar around, be careful that you don't place it below that point. Try to aim at a point where you can be about 50/50 sure about detecting differences.

There are five repetitions of the task. In each task the above mentioned random point will be different, so be vigilant on each repetition. The task is relatively fast to complete so this shouldn't take too long. You can choose yourself how much time you use per task, but I would recommend listening to at least 20 stimuli per task.
